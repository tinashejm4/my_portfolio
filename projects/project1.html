SME Loan Credit Risk Model

Financial Analytics Project | Python | Risk Analysis

This project focuses on developing a **credit risk assessment model** to estimate the probability of default for small and medium-sized enterprise (SME) loans. Financial institutions must accurately evaluate borrower risk in order to minimize loan losses while maintaining a healthy lending portfolio.

Business Problem

Banks and lending institutions face significant challenges when assessing the creditworthiness of SMEs due to limited financial history, inconsistent reporting, and industry volatility. Traditional credit assessments often rely heavily on manual judgement and limited financial ratios. This project aimed to develop a **data-driven credit risk model** that can support analysts in identifying high-risk borrowers earlier.

Business Value

Implementing a credit risk model allows financial institutions to:

• Improve loan approval accuracy • Reduce probability of default within lending portfolios • Standardize credit evaluation across analysts • Identify high-risk clients earlier in the credit lifecycle

Methodology

The model was developed using financial ratio analysis combined with statistical modelling techniques. The workflow included:

1. Data collection and cleaning of financial statement data 2. Feature engineering using key credit ratios 3. Exploratory data analysis and visualization 4. Training a predictive model to estimate default probability 5. Evaluating model performance using classification metrics

Tools & Skills Used

Python

Key Insights

The analysis revealed that liquidity ratios and interest coverage were among the strongest predictors of SME loan default. Businesses with a **Debt Service Coverage Ratio below 1.2** showed significantly higher probability of default.

Additionally, companies operating in highly cyclical industries displayed more volatile financial ratios, reinforcing the importance of incorporating industry-specific risk factors into credit models.

Next Steps

Future improvements to the model could include:

• Incorporating macroeconomic indicators • Applying machine learning classification algorithms • Building an interactive credit risk dashboard for analysts
