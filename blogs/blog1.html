Credit Risk Indicators Every Financial Analyst Should Know

By Tinashe Munikwa | March 2026 | Credit Risk Analysis

Credit risk analysis is a fundamental responsibility for financial analysts working in lending institutions, investment firms, and corporate finance teams. Understanding which indicators signal financial distress can significantly improve loan portfolio performance and investment decision-making.

Beyond the traditional metrics found in financial statements, analysts must evaluate the sustainability of earnings, liquidity positions, and operational efficiency when assessing a borrower's financial health.

1. Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio measures the borrower's ability to service debt using operating income.

DSCR = Net Operating Income / Total Debt Service

A DSCR below 1.0 indicates that the borrower does not generate enough cash flow to cover debt obligations.

2. Interest Coverage Ratio

Interest coverage assesses how comfortably a firm can meet interest payments using earnings before interest and taxes (EBIT).

Companies with a ratio below 2x often signal elevated credit risk, particularly in cyclical industries.

3. Cash Flow Quality

One of the most overlooked aspects of financial analysis is the quality of cash flows. Analysts must determine whether earnings are driven by sustainable operations or temporary accounting adjustments.

A company reporting strong earnings but weak operating cash flows may be masking deeper financial issues.

Conclusion

Effective credit analysis requires combining quantitative metrics with qualitative insights about industry conditions, management quality, and business model sustainability.

Financial analysts who develop strong risk assessment frameworks can significantly reduce default risk within lending portfolios.
